Bibimbap (Korean Rice Bowl)

Description

Bibimbap is a traditional Korean dish that features a bowl of warm white rice topped with sautéed and seasoned vegetables, sliced meat (usually beef), a fried egg, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). The ingredients are mixed together thoroughly before eating, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures. It's a nutritious and satisfying meal that can be customized with various toppings and sauces.

Ingredients

2 Servings

  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1/4 pound beef (sirloin or ribeye), thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts, blanched and drained
  • 1/2 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 zucchini, julienned
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste) for serving
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

4 Servings

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 1/2 pound beef (sirloin or ribeye), thinly sliced
  • 1 cup spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched and drained
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, julienned
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste) for serving
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

6 Servings

  • 3 cups cooked white rice
  • 3/4 pound beef (sirloin or ribeye), thinly sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
  • 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts, blanched and drained1 1/2 medium carrots, julienned
  • 1 1/2 zucchinis, julienned
  • 6 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste) for serving
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice according to package instructions and keep it warm.
  2. In a bowl, marinate the sliced beef with 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, 1 teaspoon sugar, and minced garlic. Set aside for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated beef until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add a little more oil if needed and sauté the julienned carrots and zucchini separately until tender. Season each with a pinch of salt and pepper. Remove and set aside.
  5. Blanch the spinach and bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain and squeeze out excess water. Season with a little sesame oil and salt.
  6. In a separate pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up or to your preference.
  7. To assemble the bibimbap, divide the cooked rice into four bowls. Arrange the cooked beef, spinach, bean sprouts, carrots, and zucchini on top of the rice.
  8. Place a fried egg on top of each bowl. Drizzle with the remaining sesame oil and a little soy sauce.
  9. Serve with gochujang on the side, allowing each person to add it to their taste. Garnish with sesame seeds.
  10. Mix everything together before eating to enjoy the full flavors of the dish!
