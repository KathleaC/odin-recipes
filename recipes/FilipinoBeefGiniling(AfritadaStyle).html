Filipino Beef Giniling (Afritada Style)

Description

Filipino Beef Giniling (Afritada Style) is a savory and hearty dish made with ground beef, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and peas simmered in a rich tomato sauce. This comforting meal is typically served over steamed rice and is perfect for family dinners or special occasions. The combination of tender beef and flavorful vegetables creates a delicious and satisfying dish that is loved by many.

Ingredients

3 Servings

  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 small onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 bay leaf
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell pepper (optional)

6 Servings

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper (optional)

9 Servings

  • 1 1/2 pounds ground beef
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 1/2 cups green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 1/2 small onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups tomato sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups beef broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 3/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 1/2 bay leaves
  • 3/4 cup diced red bell pepper (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
  2. Add beef and 1/2 cup water; cook and stir until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes. Season with black pepper and add more water as needed; do not let beef dry out.
  3. Add potatoes, carrots, and 3 tablespoons soy sauce; cook for 4 minutes, adding more water if mixture is too dry. Add raisins and cook for 2 to 3 minutes, again adding water as needed.
  4. Add tomato sauce, beef broth, bay leaf, and salt. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until vegetables are tender, about 15 minutes.
  5. Stir in peas and red bell pepper; cook until heated through, about 5 minutes. Remove bay leaf before serving.
